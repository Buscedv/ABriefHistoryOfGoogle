A brief history of Google

Scroll through the history of Google.

1995-2019

1995

Founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin met at Stanford Univeristy.

1996

They built a search engine called BackRub, that stood out because of it's use of links to rank webpages.

1998

BackRub was renamed to Google (a play on the word mathematical term "googol", a 1 followed by 100 zeros). The company was born when co-founder Andy Bechtolsheim wrote a check for $100,000 to "Google Inc." which until then didn't exist.

1999

Google relocated to Mountain View, and the redesign their logo.

2000

Google introduced AdWords, a self-serivce tool for creating online ad campaigns.

2003

AdSense launched as a tool for making revenue of ads on your websites.

2004

Gmail was launched on April Fool's Day.

2005

Google acquired the digital mapping company Keyhole, and soon after launched Google Maps & Google Earth.

2006

YouTube was acquired by Google.

2007

The mobile phone operating system "Android" was announced. Streetview launched in the U.S.

2008

The Google Chrome web browser was launched.

2009

Google Announces Chrome OS.

2011

Google introduced the world to Google+.

2015

Google reorganized and the holding company Alphabet Inc. was launched.

2016

The AI powered Google Assistant was launched, and Google Announces their new Smart Phone the Google Pixel.

2017

Google restructured to a limited liability company (LLC).

2019

Google shuts down Google+
